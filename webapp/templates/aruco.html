What is an ArUco Marker?

ArUco markers are square fiducial markers used in computer vision for camera calibration, pose estimation, and measuring real-world distances. Each marker has a unique binary pattern that can be detected and identified by a camera.

How to Use

  1. Select a Marker ID (any number 0–49)
  2. Choose the print size — use 6 cm for this app's default calibration
  3. Click Generate, then Print or Download
  4. Cut out the marker and tape it to a flat surface
  5. Place it in the camera's view alongside the person
  6. The app uses the marker's known size to calculate real-world distance

Tips

  • Print on white paper — avoid glossy paper (causes reflections)
  • After printing, measure the marker with a ruler to verify the size
  • Keep the marker flat — bending distorts detection
  • Ensure good lighting — avoid shadows on the marker
  • The marker should be at the same distance as the person being measured
